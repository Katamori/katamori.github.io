Genndy Tartakovsky

The creator of children's dreams


Tartakovsky in 2015, during his interview given for cartoonbrew.com
  • 1970 - He was born in Mosow.
  • 1977 - Moved to the United States (particularly Columbus, Ohio) with his family.
  • 1989 - Met Craig McCracken at California Institute of Arts.
  • 1994 - Animation director for "2 Stupid Dogs", a position he got through McCracken.
  • 1996 - "Dexter's Laboratory" started, Tartakovsky being the creator and the director of the series up to 1999.
  • 1998 - Supervising producer of "The Powerpuff Girls", for the first four seasons.
  • 2001 - Creates "Samurai Jack". Creator, director, writer, storyboard artist, producer, and executive producer.
  • 2003 - Creates "Star Wars: Clone Wars" animation series.
  • 2004 - Wins Emmy Award for "Outstanding Animated Program (For Programming One Hour or Less)" with Samurai Jack.
  • 2005 - Wins Emmy Award for "Outstanding Animated Program (For Programming One Hour or Less)" with Star Wars: Clone Wars animation series.
  • 2006 - Animation director of "Korgoth of Barbaria". The series had only one pilot episode created.
  • 2009 - Anncounced that he would gladly work on a Samurai Jack movie as a writer and director in the future.
  • 2010 - Storyboard artist of Iron Man 2.
  • 2011 - His most recent own show, "Sym-bionic Titan" is cancelled.
  • 2013 - Timing director of the pilot episode of the later Cartoon Network success, "Steven Universe".
  • 2015 - Samurai Jack movie is announced to be in production.


    • "For me, animation is the caricature of life. It's something that we create, from the ground up."

    ~ Genndy Tartakovsky
